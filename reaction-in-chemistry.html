1. Zinc and Copper Sulfate Reaction:

   Zn(s) + CuSO4(aq) → Cu(s) + ZnSO4(aq)


  Zinc (Zn) displaces copper (Cu) from copper sulfate (CuSO4) solution, resulting in the formation of copper metal and zinc sulfate (ZnSO4). Zinc is oxidized while copper is reduced.

2. Bleaching Action of Chlorine:

   Cl2(g) + H2O(l) → HCl(aq) + HClO(aq)


   Chlorine gas (Cl2) reacts with water (H2O) to produce hydrochloric acid (HCl) and hypochlorous acid (HClO), which act as strong oxidizing agents in bleaching processes.

4. Rusting of Iron:

   4Fe(s) + 3O2(g) + 6H2O(l) → 4Fe(OH)3(s)


   Iron (Fe) reacts with oxygen (O2) and water (H2O) in the presence of moisture to form iron(III) hydroxide (Fe(OH)3), commonly known as rust. Iron is oxidized in this reaction.
