Definition - Temperature unit conversion involves converting measurements of temperature from one unit to another. Temperature is a measure of the average kinetic energy of particles in a substance. It's a fundamental concept in physics, chemistry, meteorology, and many other fields. Different units of temperature are used in different parts of the world and for various applications, and conversion helps ensure accurate communication and calculations.

Types of Temperature Units



Celsius (°C):
The metric unit of temperature widely used around the world. The freezing point of water is 0°C and the boiling point is 100°C at standard atmospheric pressure.
Fahrenheit (°F):
Commonly used in the United States. The freezing point of water is 32°F, and the boiling point is 212°F.
Kelvin (K):
The SI unit of temperature. It starts from absolute zero, which is the lowest possible temperature where all molecular motion ceases.

Importance :

Temperature unit conversion is crucial in various scientific, industrial, and everyday applications. It allows for accurate communication of temperature values across different regions and systems.

 Some Basic Question


Question 1: Convert 50 degrees Celsius to Fahrenheit.

Solution :

Formula: Fahrenheit = (Celsius × 9/5) + 32
Fahrenheit = (50 × 9/5) + 32
Fahrenheit = 90 + 32
Fahrenheit = 122°F


Question 2: Convert 75 degrees Fahrenheit to Celsius.

Solution :

Formula: Celsius = (Fahrenheit - 32) × 5/9
Celsius = (75 - 32) × 5/9
Celsius = 43 × 5/9
Celsius = 23.89°C


Question 3: Convert 30 degrees Celsius to Kelvin.

Solution :

Formula: Kelvin = Celsius + 273.15
Kelvin = 30 + 273.15
Kelvin = 303.15 K


Question 4: Convert 500 Kelvin to Celsius.

Solution :

Formula: Celsius = Kelvin - 273.15
Celsius = 500 - 273.15
Celsius = 226.85°C


Question 5: Convert 20 degrees Fahrenheit to Kelvin.

Solution :

First, convert Fahrenheit to Celsius using the formula in Solution 2:
Celsius = (20 - 32) × 5/9
Celsius = -12 × 5/9
Celsius = -6.67°C

Then, convert Celsius to Kelvin using the formula in Solution 3:
Kelvin = Celsius + 273.15
Kelvin = -6.67 + 273.15
Kelvin = 266.48 K


Question 6: Convert 100 degrees Celsius to Fahrenheit.

Solution :

Formula: Fahrenheit = (Celsius × 9/5) + 32
Fahrenheit = (100 × 9/5) + 32
Fahrenheit = 180 + 32
Fahrenheit = 212°F


Question 7: Convert 68 degrees Fahrenheit to Celsius.

Solution :

Formula: Celsius = (Fahrenheit - 32) × 5/9
Celsius = (68 - 32) × 5/9
Celsius = 36 × 5/9
Celsius = 20°C


Question 8: Convert 200 Kelvin to Celsius.

Solution :

Formula: Celsius = Kelvin - 273.15
Celsius = 200 - 273.15
Celsius = -73.15°C


Question 9: Convert 95 degrees Fahrenheit to Kelvin.

Solution :

First, convert Fahrenheit to Celsius using the formula in Solution 2:
Celsius = (95 - 32) × 5/9
Celsius = 63 × 5/9
Celsius = 35°C

Then, convert Celsius to Kelvin using the formula in Solution 3:

Question 10: Convert 400 Kelvin to Fahrenheit.

Solution :

First, convert Kelvin to Celsius using the formula in Solution 4:
Celsius = 400 - 273.15
Celsius = 126.85°C

Then, convert Celsius to Fahrenheit using the formula in Solution 1:
Fahrenheit = (Celsius × 9/5) + 32
Fahrenheit = (126.85 × 9/5) + 32
