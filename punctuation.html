Punctuation

"Punctuation" refers to the marks used in writing to separate sentences and clarify meaning. Punctuation includes various symbols and marks such as periods, commas, semicolons, colons, dashes, parentheses, and quotation marks. 

The use of punctuation is essential in written language as it helps to convey meaning, indicate pauses, and clarify the relationship between different parts of a sentence. 
Proper punctuation can make writing easier to read, understand, and follow, while improper punctuation can lead to confusion or ambiguity.

Some common punctuation marks include:

Period (.) - Used to indicate the end of a sentence.
Example: "I like to read books."

Comma (,) - Used to separate items in a list, indicate pauses, or separate clauses in a sentence.
Example: "I bought apples, oranges, and bananas."

Question mark (?) - Used to indicate a direct question.
Example: "What time is it?"

Exclamation mark (!) - Used to show strong emotion or emphasis.
Example: "I can't believe it!"

Colon (:) - Used to introduce a list, an explanation, or a quotation.
Example: "There are three primary colors: red, blue, and yellow."

Semicolon (;) - Used to separate independent clauses that are closely related.
Example: "She went to the store; he stayed home."

Quotation marks (" ") - Used to indicate direct speech or to enclose a quotation.
Example: "She said, "I will be there soon."

Apostrophe (') - Used to indicate possession or contraction.
Example: "John's car" (possession), "don't" (contraction of "do not").

Dash (—) - Used to indicate a break or interruption in a sentence.
Example: "The sun was shining—bright and warm."

Parentheses ( ) - Used to enclose additional information or clarify a statement.
Example: "The conference (which was held online) was a great success."

These are just a few examples of punctuation marks. Correct and appropriate use of punctuation is essential for effective and coherent writing, as it helps convey meaning and aids in comprehension.

    Full Stop (.) :

    Full stop is used when the sentence is complete i.e. when the sentence is over then full stop (.) is put at the end of the sentence. But it is not used in interrogative and exclamatory sentences;  

    Example:

    Ram eats food.

    Ram is going to school.

    I have completed my work.

    Comma (,)

    Where there is a need to stop for a while, a short punctuation mark is used, that is, a short punctuation mark (,) is used to indicate more than one object.

    Example:

    Rama, Sita, Laksham and Hanuman are all worshiped as Gods.

    I had seen mountains, waterfalls, rivers, fields, buildings etc. in India.

    Semicolon [ ; ]

    Where there has to be a pause for a little longer than a short pause, semi-colons are used there.


    Example :

    The sun has set; Kalima took the place of Lalima.

    The sun has risen; The bird started chirping and the lotus blossomed.


    Colon [ : ]

    When a statement has to be shown separately, then incomplete stop is used there.

    Example :

    Pollution: A curse.

    Science: a boon or a curse.

      Exclamation mark-Interjection (!) :

      The exclamation mark (!) is used in a sentence to express joy, controversy, astonishment, hatred, shelter, compassion, fear, etc. That is, it is used before the adverbial word.


      Example :

      Hey! It broke.

      Hi ! He got killed.

      Ahh ! What a lovely weather.

      Wow ! What a beautiful tree.

      Question Mark [ ? ]

      A question mark is placed at the end of a sentence in which a question is asked.

      Example :

      What is your name?

      Who built Taj Mahal ?

      Bracket [ (),{},[] ]

      The word symbol used to clarify the meaning of a word. It is called parentheses.


      Example :

      Vishwamitra (trembling in anger) stop.

      Dharmaraja (Yudhishthira) was the protector of truth and dharma.

      Hyphen [ – ]

      The hyphen sign is used in the middle of all the terms.


      Example :

      Up-down, many, happiness-sorrow, profit-loss, day-night, success-failure, body-mind-wealth.

      The lovers of the country tried to protect the country with their body, mind and wealth.


      Dash [ - ]

      The sign which is used to present the description of a statement is called director.


      Example :

      Mother says- always work on time.


      Single Inverted Comma ( “…” ) :

      Used to distinguish a particular word from another.

      Example:

      'K' is a consonant.

      'A' is a vowel


      Inverted Comma ( “…” ) :

      Quotation marks or quotation marks ("...") are used to express what someone said in the same way.

      Example:

      Gandhiji said - "Do or be killed".

      Nehru ji has said – “comfort is forbidden”.

      Ram said, "Truth speaking is the biggest religion".

      Abbreviation Sign (०) :

      To write a big and famous word in brief, by writing the first letter of that word, zero (0) is put in front of it. This zero is called a short sign.


      Example:

      For Doctor – Dr.

      For Pandit – Pt.

      For Engineer – Engg.

      For Uttar Pradesh – U.P.

      Underline ( _ ) :

      Underline mark (_) is used to underline important words, phrases, sentences in any sentence.


      Example:

      Yamuna river separates Haryana and Uttar Pradesh.

      Godan novel is the best work written by Premchand.

      Krishna preached under the banyan tree.

      Omission (…) :

      When some parts are to be left out in a sentence or paragraph, then the omission mark (…) is used.


      Example:

      Ram cursed Mohan….

      I'll pick up the stuff but…

      I will definitely go home… but with you.
